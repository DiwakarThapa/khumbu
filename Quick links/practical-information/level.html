Levels of difficulty for treks



Level 1

No difficulty: example visiting Kathmandu and its surroundings.

Level 2

  • Treks usually in lodges with steps not exceeding 5 hours a day.
  • The maximum altitude reached does not exceed 4500-4700 m and no overnight above 4000 m.
  • Example: the Langtang.

Level 3

  • Treks in lodges or tents with steps of 4 to 7 hours per day and whose altitude can exceed 5000 m when passing a pass.
  • One or two nights are expected above 4000 m.
  • Example: Manaslu tower.

Level 4

  • Treks under tents or lodges and tents with steps of 6 to 9 hours a day.
  • Trekking in complete or partial autonomy with stages of several days in altitude (above 4000 m) with low temperatures.
  • Passages of passes or walk on trays to more than 5000 m.
  • Example: CB of Kangchenjunga.

HC Trek (Not Categorized)

  • Treks very demanding due to their autonomy, their duration, the length of some stages with passages over 5000 m and nights above 4500 m or 5000 m.
  • Trek requiring good endurance and a taste for effort quite pronounced.
  • In return, the welcome, kindness and sweetness of the villagers and nomads will quickly make you forget this rustic side and you will be in tune with them. The moments of exchange and sharing remain magical moments like the landscapes encountered.
  • Example: Rara Lakes trek


Levels of difficulty for trek peaks



Contrary to popular belief a trek peak is not "easy".

These are high mountain glacier routes. Good mountaineering skills and a very good physical condition are essential.

You must be equipped with all your mountaineering equipment and know how to use it: metal studs adjusted from the start on your shoes, ice ax, shoulder belt, 2 ice pins, 2 self closing safety carabiners, jumar, descender, cord, mountain helmet, frontal, survival blanket, etc.

You must be autonomous at the technical level. Being able to move uphill and downhill on slopes inclined at about 40-45 ° with Sherpas fixed ropes and you also need to know how to self-secure at a relay.

In addition to the technical problems, we must not neglect:

  • the day of the climb you will already have many days of walking, so accumulated fatigue,
  • from mid-November it is cold or very cold,
  • finally, these climbs are held at more than 6000 m.

As training, it is highly recommended to have made a race such as crossing the 3 Mont Blanc and be comfortable in this race.
