The tour of Manaslu by Larkya Pass or Tsum Valley or Rupina La

From the rice paddies to the monasteries under the eye of the Manaslu a condensed Himalaya

The tour of the Manaslu by the Larkya pass or by the Tsum Valley or after the Rupina Lasont 3 treks around the Manaslu very different.The shortest is the Manaslu trek by the Larkya Pass . Departure either from Gorkha evening of Arughat in low altitude through the rice terraces and then gradually we reach the gate of the mountain in Samagaon. Then the climb to the Larkya Pass at 5300 m arrives and takes us into a "high mountain" environment. The Rupina La is a trek whose first part is done in tents because there is no lodge. The crossing of the Rupina La pass with the descent to the Himal Chuli glacier and our camp in the pine forest are isolated places where we meet for the greatest pleasure only shepherds. The crossing of the Rupina La pass is very uncrowded.The Tsum Valley on the border of Tibet is a place of plenitude where Tibetan culture is omnipresent . Saint Milarewa meditated in the caves of this valley in the 11th century.In December and January there is the feast of horses. Races are organized with decorated horses. In May it is a religious festival celebrating the death of Buddha.It is possible for one or two days and if you wish to stay and eat at the inhabitant in the valley of Tsum.Finally, the tour of the Manaslu is 10 times less frequented than the tour of the Annapurnas.


Highlights of the treks on the Tour du Manaslu

  • The arrival and passage of the Larkya Pass.
  • Camps on the course of the Rupina La at sunset.
  • The monasteries and Tibetan culture of the Tsum valley.
  • The very typical Tibetan villages of Samagaon and Samdo
  • The views of the Manaslu from different villages at sunset and sunrise.
  • Progressive acclimatization to altitude.
