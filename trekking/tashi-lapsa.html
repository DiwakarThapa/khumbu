Tashi Lapsa Khumbu at Rolwaling



The Tashi Lapsa 5750 m

Jiri Loop through the Khumbu Valley, Tashi Lapsa and the Rolwaling Valley

The Tashi Lapsa is not a pass like the other passes of Khumbu: the Chola Pass or the Renjo Pass. It is a more difficult trek. Cross the Tashi Lapsa request to have the tents and 2 camps are needed at more than 5000 m. The stages of departure are quite long. Tashi Lapsa connects the Khumbu Valley with the Rolwaling Valley . Compared to the other passes of Khumbu it is much less frequented. We offer you a departure from Jiri . During the first 5 days of Jiri on the path between Lukla and Namche it is only happiness. Few people, paths balcony, rhododendrons in the spring, all in a setting that looks at times the southern Alps. Then we go up the Khumbu valley to Thame. After that it's crossing the Tashi Lapsa. From Thame to Na it is a very high mountain atmosphere. Landscapes totally different from those previously crossed with peaks of more than 6000 m snow-covered nearby. We move on glaciers and the camp of Phu Cave at 5650 m offers us at sunset an exceptional belvedere. After crossing the Tashi Lapsa we are in the Rolwaling another Sherpa valley that will allow us to reach a village near our starting point Jiri. Still a little used area.


Highlights of the Tashi Lapsa trek from Khumbu to Rolwaling

  • Variety of landscapes from Jiri Solo Khumbu to the high mountains at Tashi Lapsa and the remote area of ​​Rolwaling.
  • A loop course on two valleys that Solo Khumbu and the Rolwaling.
  • Course much less frequented than the Khumbu passes.
  • Several stages at the inhabitant.
  • The sunset at Phu Cave from the tent at 5600 m.
  • The passage of Tashi Lapsa at 5750 m with the view of the Everest massif.
